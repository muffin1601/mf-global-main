CRM Audit Report

Prepared for the Business Owner  |  15 June 2026
Independent review of the CRM application against the official "CRM Architecture Guide" presentation.

Bottom line: What has been built is a good Lead-Management and Quotation tool — but not the full CRM described in the guide. Three of a CRM's core pillars (Accounts, Contacts, and a Sales Pipeline / Deals) do not exist, and the advanced features (automation, forecasting, territories, approvals, email workflows) are missing.

Launch verdict: Not ready to launch as a "CRM." It is usable today as a lead tool only after one critical fix (see Section 3). Overall completion is about 36 out of 100.

3. Most Important Finding

The dashboard's Revenue / Profit / Leads charts display fake, hard-coded numbers. Anyone viewing the dashboard sees impressive-looking graphs that are not connected to real data. This is the single most urgent issue, because it can mislead management and customers. It should be fixed (or clearly labelled "sample data") before any demo or launch.

6. Quick Wins (Fast, High-Value Fixes)

  1. Replace the fake dashboard charts with real data — small effort.
  2. Capture a "reason" when a lead is marked Won or Lost — small.
  3. Make tasks assignable and link them to leads — small to medium.
  4. Add automatic follow-up reminder emails — medium.
  5. Add an email alert when a lead is assigned — small.
  6. Add a value field to leads so pipeline figures mean something — small.
  7. Add failure alerts for the automatic lead-import jobs — small.

7. Bonus Features Found (not in the guide)

  • Automatic lead import from IndiaMart & TradeIndia (every 5 minutes).
  • Vendor management and a product catalogue with tiered pricing.
  • A full quotation PDF generator (GST/TCS, bank details).
  • A bundled marketing website, blog, and visitor tracking.
  • Fine-grained per-lead permissions (more advanced than the guide required).

8. Can the Sales Team Use It Today?

Partly. The team can capture, assign, call-track, follow up on leads, and send professional quotations — that daily flow works. They cannot manage company accounts, run a deal pipeline, forecast revenue, or trust the dashboard charts.

Compared to Zoho CRM (free/basic) or HubSpot CRM (starter): No — both of those include Accounts, Contacts, Deals/Pipeline, and email tools that this product lacks. However, this product beats both on India-specific lead-portal auto-import and GST quotations.

9. Final Recommendation

As a full "CRM" (per the guide): Not ready — missing Accounts, Contacts, Deals, and all advanced features.

As a Lead-Management + Quotation tool: Ready with fixes — usable once the fake dashboard charts are corrected and follow-up reminders are added.

Recommended path: Launch the current strengths now as a "Lead Management" product (after fixing the charts), and fund a Phase 2 to add Accounts, Contacts, and Deals/Pipeline to become a true CRM.
